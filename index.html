About Me

Currently pursuing a Master's in Visual Computing at the University of Siegen, Germany — specialising in computer vision, deep learning, and 3D graphics.

Deep learning, computational geometry, and autonomous systems are where my work sits. What draws me in is the mathematical elegance underneath it all — how structures like quaternions, splines, and tensor fields let machines reason about 3D space in ways that mirror how we do.

Before Siegen, I completed a B.Sc.(Honours) in Computer Science at Delhi University and worked as a Data Analyst at Enlearn Academy. Outside of academia I keep building — implementing papers from scratch, exploring procedural generation, and writing clean, well-documented code.

Trilinear Interpolation

Implementation of trilinear interpolation for estimating values at arbitrary 3D points within a rectangular grid. Includes derivation from bilinear interpolation, applied in volume rendering and 3D texture sampling pipelines.

Python NumPy 3D Graphics

Quaternion 3D Rotations

Mathematical implementation of quaternion algebra for representing and composing 3D rotations — avoiding gimbal lock. Covers SLERP interpolation, conversion from/to Euler angles and rotation matrices, with applications in skeletal animation.

Python C++ Linear Algebra
